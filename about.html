ABOUT AIR

One crew. Two runways.

Most studios pick a lane — web shop or app shop. We built AIR around the belief that good engineering and good taste transfer across both.

AIR exists because products don't stay in one lane anymore — an app needs a marketing site, a platform needs both.

We started AIR to close the gap between "who builds our website" and "who builds our app" — two conversations that, for most founders, shouldn't need to be separate at all.

Every project runs through the same flight plan — brief, build, test, launch — whether it lands on a browser tab or a home screen. That consistency is the whole pitch: fewer handoffs, fewer translation errors, one team accountable for the outcome.

What we hold to

The values behind the build.

01 Ship in the open

Every sprint ends with something you can click through, not a status update. Progress you can see beats progress you're told about.

02 One point of contact

You get a single accountable lead across the whole build, even when the work spans design, engineering, and QA.

03 Build for the edge cases

Slow networks, small screens, weird input devices. We test for the conditions your best users won't hit, not just the demo path.

04 Taste is a technical skill

Performance and polish aren't a final pass — they're part of the brief from day one, on every surface we build for.
